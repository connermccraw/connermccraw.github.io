My training in physiological assessment began with the Vascular Biology and Autonomic Systems Laboratory at Appalachian Sate University. There, I evaluated the analytical validity of a suite of Withings biometric monitoring technologies against laboratory gold-standard assessments of pulse-wave velocity, sleep, body composition, energy expenditure, and more. Garnering these data required API calls, so this was the genesis of my programming journey.

After completing my graduate studies in 2018, I joined POTENTRx, a Seattle-based startup, where I ran a cardiopulmonary diagnostic stress testing laboratory and integrated data from connected health devices to inform individualized lifestyle medicine programs for chronic disease management and prevention.

Transitioning to full-time software development was the natural next step because through my time in research and healthcare I realized how empowering wearables and connected health devices could be for patients. Seeing more of their "ah ha" moments from health data that's generated and represented in a meaningful way became my itch.
